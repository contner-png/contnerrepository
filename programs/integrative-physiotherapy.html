Clinical Program 02

Integrative Physiotherapy

Pain is not merely a mechanical tissue issue; it is a complex output generated by the nervous system. Operating strictly on the Biopsychosocial (BPS) Model of Pain, our DPT evaluates the complex interplay between biological tissue capacity, psychological stress, and environmental factors. We utilize graded exposure, pain neuroscience education, and movement optimization to down-regulate a hypersensitive nervous system and build robust, resilient human movement.

Our Different Treatment Strategy

Rather than chasing transient "trigger points" or providing passive modalities that fail to build long-term capacity, our approach is focused on systemic load-bearing tolerance. We understand that your body is a dynamic system capable of profound adaptation.

Our physical therapy protocols integrate graded exposure therapy to safely increase tissue capacity and Pain Neuroscience Education (PNE) to provide patients with an evidence-based framework for understanding their pain. By shifting the focus from "tissue damage" to "nervous system sensitivity," we facilitate a state of readiness that allows for peak physical performance.
