PEMF (Pulsed Electromagnetic Field) Therapy

A restorative, non-invasive therapy that uses mild, pulsing magnetic fields to support cellular function and optimize recovery.

Mechanism of Action

The Science

Healthy human cells rely on a specific electrical charge (transmembrane potential) to absorb nutrients and expel waste. Inflammation and cellular stress can degrade this voltage. PEMF induces a targeted electromagnetic field that helps optimize this cellular charge, supporting efficient ion exchange and healthy cellular metabolism.

Clinical Application

Why We Use It

We utilize PEMF as a systemic "reset." It is widely supported in clinical literature for facilitating bone density and tissue health, and we employ it to help shift the central nervous system out of a chronic state of stress into a restorative, parasympathetic state.
